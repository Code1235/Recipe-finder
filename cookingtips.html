Cooking Tips

1. Read the Recipe Thoroughly First Always read through the entire recipe before starting.This helps you understand the steps and ensure you have all ingredients and tools ready.

2. Mise en Place (Everything in its Place) Prepping all your ingredients before starting to cook makes the process smoother. Chop vegetables, measure out spices, and get all utensils and pans ready.

3. Season as You Go Add salt and seasonings gradually as you cook to build layers of flavor,rather than adding them all at once or at the end.

4. Taste While Cooking Tasting your food as you cook helps you adjust seasoning and make sure it’s on track for the final flavor.

5. Use Fresh Herbs at the End Adding fresh herbs like basil, cilantro, or parsley at the end of cooking preserves their fresh, vibrant flavor.

6. Preheat the Pan or Oven Always preheat your oven or pan for even cooking. This helps with caramelization and prevents sticking.

7. Don’t Overcrowd the Pan When frying or roasting, leave space around your food. Overcrowding traps steam and prevents browning.

8. Let Meat Rest After cooking meat, let it rest for a few minutes before cutting into it. This allows the juices to redistribute for a more flavorful and tender result.

9. Proper Knife Skills Learn basic knife techniques to speed up your prep work and ensure safety in the kitchen. A sharp knife is safer than a dull one.

10. Deglaze the Pan After sautéing or searing, pour some liquid (like wine, broth, or vinegar) into the hot pan to scrape up the flavorful browned bits and make a delicious sauce.
